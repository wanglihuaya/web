Commencement address, 2005, Stanford University (excerpt)

Steve Jobs

I am honored to be with you today at your commencement from one of the finest universities in the world. I never graduated from college. To be honest, this is the closest I've ever gotten to a college graduation. Today I want to tell you three stories from my life. No big deal, just three stories.

The first story is about connecting the dots.

I dropped out of Reed College after the first 6 months, but then stayed around as a drop-in for another 18 months or so before I really quit. Why did I drop out?

The story begins when I was born. My biological mother was a young, unmarried college graduate. She decided to put me up for adoption. She really wanted me to be adopted by college graduates. So when I was born,she had made all the preparations. So my parents got a call in the middle of the night asking: "We have an unexpected baby boy; do you want him?" They replied:

"Of course!" My biological mother later found out that my mother had never graduated from college and that my father had never graduated from high school. She refused to sign the adoption contract. She only reluctantly agreed a few months later when my parents promised that I would go to college.

At seventeen, I did go to college. But I foolishly chose a college that was almost as expensive as Stanford. My working-class parents spent almost all of their savings on my college tuition. After six months, I couldn't see the value in it. I had no idea what I really wanted to do and no idea how college was going to help me figure it out. But here I was spending almost all of my parents' life savings. So I decided to drop out. I thought it was the right decision. I can't deny that I was scared, but looking back it was one of the best decisions of my life. The minute I dropped out I could stop taking the classes that didn't interest me. Then I can start taking courses that look interesting.
